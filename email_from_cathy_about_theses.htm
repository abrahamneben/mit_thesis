Checklist for PhD students anticipating graduation:

File a degree application:

Each of you has already done this.  Students on the September and February degree lists may participate in Commencement the following June.

Planning your thesis defense:

You are responsible for scheduling the thesis defense and arranging for the room in which it will take place; the various research areas each have rooms that their students generally use for defenses.  START EARLY to arrange scheduling:  it’s often said that one of the hardest parts of the PhD program is getting all three faculty members on your committee into the same room at the same time!  Once you have the date, time, and place established, please let Sydney Miller (sydney@mit.edu) know the specifics, and confirm your final thesis title with her as well.  [Sydney is serving as temporary Graduate Assistant in the wake of Sabrina Salvati’s resignation.  We’ll be hiring a new permanent staff member soon.]  Sydney will send notice to the Physics community, including to all faculty members, as your defense is open to the public.

When should your defense be?  You’ll find that the Registrar’s official Academic Calendar lists dates by which your thesis must be submitted, but these are official dates, and in practice we have some discretion to accept your thesis later than the Registrar’s date.  For June graduates, Friday, May 5 is this year’s official deadline.  However, unofficially we can accept your finished thesis two to three weeks later than this date, by individual arrangement between you and us.  The absolute final day we can accept your thesis is Friday, May 26, 2017.

But that’s your finished thesis, meaning that you are giving us a formally printed copy, on library-specified paper and covers, signed by you and your advisor.  First you will need to submit a draft to your committee, who should let you know how soon before the defense they want to see it – generally this is about two weeks.  You also need to consider that it’s likely you will have revisions your committee requires you to make to the written thesis following your defense.  We recommend that you give yourself at least two weeks between the day of your defense and the day you intend to turn the thesis in.  So, from the time you finish your draft for the committee to the time you submit the final version to the Academic Programs Office, try to budget a full four weeks. 

 

 

And keep in mind: 

On the day you turn in your thesis, we are required to report to the Physics Fiscal Office that you have completed all the work for your PhD, and your graduate appointment will then be cancelled at one of two points in the month:  on the 15th, if you have turned your thesis in between the first and fifteenth of the month, or on the final day of the month, if you have turned it in between the sixteenth and the last day of the month.  If you are finishing close to the end of the term, we will trust that you are taking the time you need to finish your revisions, and will only insist on your turning in the final version as the deadline approaches.  If your schedule is such that you will defend and finish your revisions any more than four weeks before the end of the term, we will expect to receive it within two to three weeks after your defense.

Moving on to a postdoc at MIT?

 

If you plan to work at MIT immediately after your student status ends, please let Sydney and me know this as soon as possible.  There are a variety of administrative processes that have to be followed before you can be cleared to be hired as an MIT employee, especially if you are an international student, and the timing can be tricky.  And if you are applying for OPT, please let us know now so that we can work on the details with you.  In general, the sooner we know your plans, the better.

What to prepare:

The package of your finished thesis includes:

1.     two original copies on thesis archival paper with original signatures (yours and your supervisor’s, and co-supervisor’s if you have one.  We will obtain the Associate Head’s signature, but you should put the name Nergis Mavalvala and the title “Associate Head” on the title page) and

2.     the completed form from the Specifications for Thesis Preparation, with an abstract and unsigned title page attached.

You are responsible for determining whether your supervisor, fellowship sponsor, or any other party requires an additional copy of your thesis.

You’ll find details of the required thesis, abstract, and title page formats in Specifications for Thesis Preparation.  Please follow these specifications to the letter.  Our friends in the MIT Libraries have also put together a helpful FAQ page on preparing your thesis. 

Be aware that a thesis archival fee will be charged to your student account.  The Institute requires that this, and all outstanding charges, be paid before your final degree is approved.

 

Documentation of your degree:

You will receive your formal diploma at Commencement if you are attending; you also had the opportunity to arrange with the Registrar (when you filed your Degree Application) to have your diploma held or sent to you, if you are not attending Commencement.

Students about to graduate often need to provide documentation to an employer that they will shortly be completing their PhD.  We can produce letters for this purpose in the Academic Programs Office.  Ideally you’ll give us a few days’ notice, but we can often create them in less time if necessary.
